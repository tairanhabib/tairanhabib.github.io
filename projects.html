Loop-Closing Vine Robot2025–now
Pneumatic soft robotics for healthcare · MIT d'Arbeloff Lab

Developing a vine robot that forms closed loops to gently grasp delicate objects, aimed at healthcare use. Built the full-stack control integrating crane and vine subsystems across Arduino, Raspberry Pi, and VMs with SMC regulators and pneumatic pouch motors. Coded X/Y directional control using pouch motors at 120° intervals and NumPy-based arrays for autonomous crane positioning across all degrees of freedom, plus custom PWM converter interfaces for the regulators. Now adding vision-based feedback control for positioning. Demo: PS4-controller teleoperation of the arm via pouch motors.
